Transfers & Promotions · Page 4

72 IAS Officers: Gujarat's Mass Transfer Order of 13 May 2026

Staff Reporter·June 2026 Issue

In one of the largest administrative reshuffles in recent years, the Gujarat government issued a mass transfer order on 13 May 2026 affecting 72 IAS officers across the state. The order included major changes at the Secretariat level, with 21 officers transferred and 39 promoted to the rank of Deputy Secretary.

Additionally, 37 officers from the State Forest Service (SFS) were promoted to the Indian Forest Service (IFS) cadre through the UPSC Select List 2025, marking a significant career milestone for state service officers.

NPS / Pension · RTI · Service Rules · Page 8–16

NPS Family Pension — Complete Eligibility Guide

Sri Haresh Joshi·Retired Dy. Director (Accounts)

Under the National Pension Scheme (NPS), family members of deceased government employees are entitled to pension benefits under specific conditions. This guide, prepared by retired Dy. Director of Accounts Sri Haresh Joshi, covers the full eligibility criteria, documentation required, and the application process for Gujarat government employees.

DA Increased to 60% effective 01/01/2026 — arrears of 4 months to be credited before July 2026

RTI Case: The Information Commission imposed a personal fine of ₹10,000 on the Mamlatdar in the Aravalli case for failure to provide information under RTI within the stipulated 30-day period. This landmark ruling reinforces the obligation of public information officers.

Casual Leave Rule (GR dated 06-02-1961): Government employees may avail up to 8 consecutive days of casual leave at one time. The IFMS "Pull Back" feature has been removed — employees should verify their bills before final submission as the reversal option is no longer available.

ACB Cases · Court Judgments · Page 20–27

ACB Trap Cases — June 2026 Across Gujarat

Staff Reporter·ACB / Vigilance

The Anti-Corruption Bureau recorded multiple trap cases across Gujarat in the month of June 2026. Cases were filed in Anand (hospital sector), Gandhinagar (department official), Bharuch (Talati), and other districts. Names, posts, bribe amounts, and current status are detailed in the full print issue.

⚖️ Gujarat HC Judgments — Summary

Compulsory Retirement Case (Sept 2025 judgment): HC ruled on the validity of compulsory retirement orders — key implications for employees facing disciplinary action.

Daily-Wage Regularisation (March 2026 judgment): Landmark verdict on the rights of daily-wage employees seeking regularisation — applicable across Gujarat departments.

Contractual Counsellors Case: Status of contractual counsellors employed in government schools — job security ruling.
